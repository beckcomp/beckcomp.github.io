About Me

I am an MSc student in Computer Science at the Institute of Informatics (INF) of the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS) and a Medical Student at the Federal University of Health Sciences of Porto Alegre (UFCSPA)

My current research is in the fields of Digital Signal Processing, more specifically on sampling theory, where I explore methods for spectral recovery of sub-Nyquist sampled signals.

I graduated summa cum laude in Computer Science from UFRGS in 2024. As an undergraduate student, I was also a researcher in the Parallel and Distributed Processing Group (GPPD), where I worked for about two years on a project with Petrobras in the fields of Computer Graphics, Computational Geophysics and High-Performance Computing.

As a graduate student, I am a member of the IEEE UFRGS Student Branch and, as a medical student, I am a member of the UFCSPA Human Anatomy League and the UFCSPA Ophthalmology League.

I have a strong interest in linguistics and I study foreign languages as a hobby. I hold a C2 level of English and I'm currently studying Japanese. In the past, I have studied Korean, Chinese, Spanish, and French as well.

My favourite languages are C, C++, and Japanese.
